## Results  
- The unauthorized VPN login attempts were successfully detected and blocked before account compromise occurred.
- MFA protection prevented successful authentication despite the attacker possessing valid credentials.
- Investigation confirmed that Monica was physically present in the office during the attack, ruling her out as the originator of the VPN activity.
- Multiple failed login attempts indicated potential brute-force or automated attack behavior.
- Threat intelligence analysis showed that the source IP was not present in known malicious databases, suggesting the possible use of a compromised residential IP address.

## Remediation  
- Reset Monica’s compromised credentials immediately.
- Enforce strong password policies and password rotation.
- Maintain MFA enforcement for all VPN access.
- Block or monitor suspicious source IP addresses.
- Restrict VPN access from unauthorized geographic regions.
- Conduct phishing awareness training for employees.
- Monitor for repeated failed authentication attempts.
- Enable alerting for suspicious login patterns and impossible travel activity.
- Review VPN access logs regularly for unauthorized access attempts.
